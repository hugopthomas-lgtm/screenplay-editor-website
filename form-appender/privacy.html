Form Appender, Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 12, 2026

1. Overview

Form Appender is a Google Forms add-on developed by Hugo Thomas. It automatically appends form submission responses to Google Docs that you select through the Google Picker. This privacy policy explains what data the add-on accesses, how that data is handled and protected, and your rights regarding your data.

2. Limited Use of Google user data

Form Appender's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically:

  • We do not use Google user data for serving advertisements.
  • We do not sell, rent, or trade Google user data to any third party.
  • We do not transfer Google user data to any third party, except as necessary to provide the add-on's features (all such processing happens client-side within Google's own infrastructure).
  • Human review of Google user data is performed only when (a) you give explicit consent for a specific request, (b) it is necessary for security or to comply with applicable law, or (c) the data is aggregated and used for internal operations in accordance with the Limited Use requirements.

3. Data the add-on accesses

Form Appender accesses the following data, only to perform its stated function:

  • Google Forms data (current form only) — question titles, question types, and the responses submitted by respondents. Used to format the appended content.
  • Google Drive (only files and folders you explicitly select via the Google Picker) — to read the names of Google Docs inside the folder you selected, and to write to the destination Doc(s) you selected.
  • Google Docs — read and write access to the destination Doc(s) you selected, to append formatted responses.
  • Respondent email address — included in the appended output only if you enable the option "Include respondent email" in the sidebar.
Form Appender uses the narrow drive.file scope. This means the add-on cannot see, list, or access any file or folder in your Drive other than what you explicitly choose through the Google Picker. We never have a blanket view of your Drive.

4. Data we do not access

  • Other Google Forms in your account.
  • Drive files or folders you have not selected via the Picker.
  • Your contacts, calendar, Gmail, or any other Google product.
  • Your name, profile picture, or other personal identifiers beyond the optional respondent email.

5. Data storage

The only data Form Appender stores is your add-on configuration: the IDs and names of the folder and Doc(s) you selected, the form-question selections, format preferences, and processing statistics (submission count, last run time, last error message).

This configuration is stored using Google Apps Script's DocumentProperties, which is scoped to the specific Form on which the add-on is installed. It is stored entirely within Google's infrastructure and is not visible to the developer or to any third party.

Form Appender has no external backend, no database, and no analytics. No data ever leaves Google's servers as a result of using this add-on.

6. Data protection and security

All data accessed by Form Appender is processed inside Google's own infrastructure (Google Apps Script runtime, Google Drive, Google Docs, Google Forms). Specifically:

  • Encryption in transit: all communication between your browser, the Google Apps Script runtime, and Google APIs uses HTTPS / TLS as enforced by Google.
  • Encryption at rest: configuration data stored in DocumentProperties and all referenced Forms, Drive folders, and Docs are encrypted at rest by Google in accordance with Google's data security standards.
  • Authentication: access to your data is gated by Google's OAuth 2.0 flow. The add-on can only act on your behalf after you have granted the requested scopes through Google's consent screen.
  • Authorization scope minimization: the add-on requests the narrowest scopes that allow it to function. It uses drive.file rather than broader Drive scopes, so the add-on can only access files and folders you have explicitly authorized through the Google Picker.
  • No third-party transfer: the add-on does not transmit your data to any external server, database, log, or analytics service controlled by the developer or by any third party.
  • Code distribution: the add-on is distributed exclusively via the Google Workspace Marketplace. Code updates go through Google's review process.
  • Developer access: the developer (Hugo Thomas) does not have access to your Drive files, Forms responses, or any personal data. The developer can only inspect aggregated, anonymous error logs produced by Google Apps Script's Stackdriver integration if you choose to share them for support.

7. OAuth scopes explained

  • forms.currentonly — read the questions and listen for new submissions on the current form only.
  • documents — read and write the destination Google Doc(s) you select via the Picker.
  • drive.file — read metadata (name, type) of the folder and Doc(s) you select via the Google Picker, and list Google Docs contained in the folder you selected. The add-on cannot see other Drive items.
  • script.container.ui — display the configuration sidebar inside the Forms editor.
  • script.scriptapp — install and manage the on-submit trigger so the add-on can react to new responses.

8. Data retention and deletion

Form Appender's configuration data persists as long as the Form on which it is installed exists. You can delete all stored configuration and statistics at any time by:

  • Clicking the "Réinitialiser la config" / "Reset configuration" button in the sidebar.
  • Uninstalling the add-on from the Form (Forms menu → Add-ons → Manage add-ons).
  • Deleting the Form itself.

You can revoke the add-on's access to your Google account at any time at https://myaccount.google.com/permissions. Once access is revoked, the add-on cannot read any of your data.

9. Children's privacy

Form Appender is not directed at children under 13. The developer does not knowingly collect personal data from children.
