Short Film Screenplay Template for Google Docs

A free template designed for films under 40 minutes

Short films demand tight storytelling. Every scene, every line of dialogue, every action must earn its place on the page. This template gives you the proper formatting foundation so you can focus on crafting a story that works within a limited runtime.

Writing a short film isn't just writing a shorter feature. The format requires a different approach: faster setups, fewer characters, and stories that can be told in 5, 10, or 20 pages. Film festivals, student projects, and proof-of-concept shorts all benefit from scripts that understand these constraints.

This Google Docs template follows industry-standard screenplay formatting while being optimized for the short form. Whether you're entering a 48-hour film competition or developing a passion project, you'll have a professional foundation from page one.

Short Film-Specific Formatting Tips

Start Late, End Early

Short films don't have time for lengthy setups. Begin your scenes as late as possible—often mid-conversation or mid-action. End them the moment the dramatic point is made. This keeps your page count lean and your story moving.

Limit Your Locations

Every new location costs time and money in production. For short films, aim for 1-3 locations maximum. Use your scene headings strategically—multiple scenes in the same location are easier to shoot and often create stronger visual continuity.

Trim Your Cast

Two or three characters are often enough for a powerful short. Each character name in your script represents a casting decision, a costume, and screen time. Fewer characters means deeper development within your limited pages.

Embrace Visual Storytelling

Short films often work best with minimal dialogue. Use your action lines to tell the story visually. A glance, a gesture, an object in frame—these can replace entire conversations and keep your page count under control.
