Industry Advice

They Do Read. Here's How to Reach Them.

The myth that producers don't read unsolicited scripts is mostly false. For short films, the door is more open than you think.

The Method

How I Found Producers for My Shorts

This isn't theory. This is exactly what I did, starting from zero connections.

  1. Find festivals you'd love to screen at. Start with the ones you've heard of, then dig into the ones that match your taste.
  2. Look at their winners and selections. Check the last few years.
  3. For each film, find the production company. It's usually in the credits or on IMDb.
  4. Find their email. Most production companies have a contact page. Some are harder to find, but they exist.
  5. Send a sharp email. Not a novel. Not a generic pitch. Something personal, with proof that you've done the work.
An Email That Worked

Real Email, Real Results

Here's an actual email I sent in 2014, when I was just out of film school. It led to real conversations.

Subject: Short film

Hello,

I'm reaching out because I just graduated from the first class of Luc Besson's film school, where I studied directing.

I've already written and directed a self-produced short, Premier Metro (7 min), which was selected at 10 festivals and won 4 awards. My second short, Perrault, La Fontaine, Mon Cul! (18 min), made as my graduation film, was noticed by Luc Besson himself, who gave it a 17/20.

I'm currently finishing my first feature screenplay, L'hiver, and I'm looking for a production company like yours — one that produces both shorts and features, especially in the dramatic comedy genre. I'd love to direct my first produced short, Une Place Pour Les Choses.

Attached: the screenplay, my CV, and links to my two shorts.

Best,
Hugo
