Horror Writing Tips

Craft Fear on the Page

Horror screenwriting has its own rules. Here's how to make readers feel the dread.

White Space is Your Weapon

Short paragraphs. Single-line actions. Let the page breathe. Empty space creates anticipation. When readers see white space, they slow down. They feel the dread building.

Sound Before Sight

What we hear is often scarier than what we see. A creak. A whisper. Breathing in the dark. Let sound design do the heavy lifting in your action lines.

Delay the Reveal

The monster you don't see is always scarier. Keep your creature, killer, or threat in the shadows as long as possible. Build anticipation through glimpses and sounds.

Make Us Care First

Horror without empathy is just gore. Give us characters we root for before you put them in danger. The scares hit harder when we care about survival.
