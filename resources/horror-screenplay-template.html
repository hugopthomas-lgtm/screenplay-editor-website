Why this template?

Horror is rhythm. It's knowing when to hold a beat, when to break the silence, when to make the audience look away — and when to make them regret it.

This template is built around the structure that made Scream a masterclass in tension: the setup, the false relief, the real scare. It's not about jump scares. It's about making readers feel unsafe on the page before anything even happens.

What's inside

Tension structure

How to build dread that pays off. Setup, escalation, release — the rhythm that keeps readers on edge.

White space as a weapon

Horror lives in what you don't show. Learn to use the page itself to create unease.

The film behind the template

Scream (1996) didn't just revive the slasher genre — it rewrote the rules. Kevin Williamson's script is a masterclass in misdirection, self-awareness, and the art of making an audience complicit in the horror.

The opening scene with Drew Barrymore is twelve minutes of pure tension. No backstory, no exposition — just a phone call, a quiz, and the slow realization that the rules have changed. That's the energy this template captures.

Study Scream. Then break its rules on purpose.
